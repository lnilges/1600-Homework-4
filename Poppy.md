# Poppy

### Poppies are vibrant flowers that are known for their cultural significance and medicianl uses. Poppies are associated with rememberance, the red poppy is the symbol of rememberance for soldiers who dies in World War 1. For medicinal uses, the opium derived from the opium poppy has been used for pain relief and as a sedative. 

Common Poppy Species:
- Opium Pippy
- California Poppy
- Oriental Poppy
- Iceland Poppy

Poppies can be annual, biennial, or short-lived and typically have four to six petals. Common color varieties include pink, red, white, and yellow. They can grow to over 1 meter tall and usually bloom in spring and early summer.

### Care Tips:
1. Poppies need 6 or more hours of sunlight daily
2. Well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0 is needed
3. Water flowers weekly and adjust watering amount based on yellowing or wilting leaves
4. Place poppy seeds at least 12 to 18 inches apart to reduce competition and promote airflow
